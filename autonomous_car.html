Autonomous Car

During a the lab for a semester-long course on Robotics and Intelligent Systems, I and 3 lab partners programmed an RC truck in Python, using Robotic Operating System (ROS), to complete sevel path planning tasks. The course culminated in a two-part final project. In Part 1, we implemented ILQR with cost-prioritization, enabling the truck to safely race along a track with randomly placed obstacles. In Part 2, we implemented a safety filter to override unsafe driver inputs like steering off the track, running into obstacles, and veering into a lane with oncoming traffic.

The image above displays the first vertical seam (in red and on the right side of the image) that is removed from the original image. The image is grayscale as it shows the energy levels of each pixel. The whiter the pixel, the more energy it has. As stated above, the seam that is carved from the image is the seam with the lowest total energy.

The images below are the original image, and then the image with 100 seams removed from both the vertical and horizontal directions. The differences between the two images are clear when one compares each edge of the photos.
