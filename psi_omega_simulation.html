Psi-Omega Numerical Heat Transfer Simulation

This computational fluid dynamics project explored unsteady heat transfer in low-Reynolds-number flows using the streamfunction-vorticity finite-difference method. I simulated two key scenarios: convective flow around a circular cylinder and flow over a NACA 2412 airfoil from a Cessna 182 aircraft.

Technical Approach

I implemented the Psi-Omega (streamfunction-vorticity) method from scratch in MATLAB, solving three coupled governing equations: the Poisson equation for streamfunction, the momentum equation for vorticity evolution, and the energy equation for temperature distribution. The simulation used upwind differencing for spatial derivatives and forward Euler time advancement, with Gauss-Seidel iteration and over-relaxation for faster convergence.

Key Results & Validation

Cylinder Flow

Successfully generated characteristic vortex shedding that visually matched reference simulations, though Strouhal numbers of 0.02 and 0.42 deviated from the expected ~0.2 standard. Heat transfer results aligned with expectations, maintaining proper temperature boundaries (400°C cylinder, 300°C flow) and achieving 7.6 kW/m² heat flux.

Airfoil Analysis

The 5-degree simulation produced smooth, attached flow with no vortex shedding as expected, validating perfectly against SimFlow CFD software. At 30 degrees, distinct vortex shedding confirmed stall conditions with a Strouhal number of 0.62. The reduced heat flux (1,002.6 W/m² vs 1,133.6 W/m²) validated expected wake shielding effects. While visual flow patterns and heat transfer trends matched theoretical expectations excellently, the cylinder Strouhal discrepancy suggested areas for future parameter refinement. The airfoil simulations demonstrated strong agreement with both physical expectations and independent validation.

Performance Against Standards

While visual flow patterns and heat transfer trends matched theoretical expectations and reference data excellently, the cylinder Strouhal numbers suggested potential areas for parameter refinement in future work. The airfoil simulations demonstrated excellent agreement with both physical expectations and independent CFD validation, confirming the robustness of my numerical implementation for complex geometries.

Technical Challenges & Learning Outcomes

The most significant challenge was code debugging and optimization, requiring systematic troubleshooting through 10 code iterations for Part A and 5 for Part B to resolve numerical stability issues and matrix indexing errors. With simulations requiring 3-6 hours of runtime, computational efficiency became critical.

This project strengthened my computational mathematics foundation through implementing finite-difference schemes and iterative solvers, while developing robust MATLAB programming skills with proper boundary conditions. Most importantly, watching vortex shedding patterns and temperature fields evolve in real-time provided invaluable physical insights that analytical solutions alone cannot offer. The extensive debugging process taught systematic problem-solving approaches essential for complex numerical simulations, creating a comprehensive learning experience that bridges academic concepts with practical engineering applications.
